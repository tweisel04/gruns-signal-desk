Data integrity & robustness.

A dashboard built on weak data is worse than no dashboard. Every signal in the Signal Desk is audited weekly across six dimensions. This page shows the audit, the open issues, and the caveats a finance team should know about before acting on the forecast.
92/100
Composite Integrity
GRADE A · TRUSTWORTHY

Signals are directionally reliable, with two open caveats.

Across 24 automated tests covering sampling, measurement, model, and drift, the Signal Desk passes 21, flags 2 as warnings, and has 1 open issue under active remediation. The forecast model's 60-day estimate carries an honest confidence band; the panel is well-stratified; social listening precision/recall are within SLA. Two areas to watch before making large resource decisions on model output:

  • PASSPanel stratification — weighted to US census on age, income, region; 89% wave-to-wave retention; no detectable Grüns-user bias in recruitment flow.
  • PASSSocial listening precision — 94% on hand-labeled sample (target ≥90%); "grüns" false-match rate held below 3% after German/vegetable filter added.
  • WARNFeature multicollinearity in forecast — social velocity and branded search correlate at r=0.71. Driver attribution may overstate social contribution by 15-25%. Under orthogonalization in v2.5.
  • WARNThin SKU samples — Watermelon trial has 124 reviews, below the 300-review threshold for stable grading. Treat the "C" grade as directional, not definitive.
  • OPENReflexivity risk unquantified — if Grüns acts on dashboard signals (e.g., pauses TikTok spend), the training distribution shifts. No counterfactual backtest yet. Scheduled for Q2.

Known caveats / things to say out loud before acting on the forecast
plain-english version
01
Forecast driver attribution may overstate social contribution. Social velocity and branded search move together (r=0.71), so the model can credit the same underlying buzz twice. Treat the "+4.2k from TikTok" number as a directional ceiling; the true causal attribution is probably 70-85% of that. Fix in progress: orthogonalizing features in v2.5 via residualization.
MEDIUM
02
Reflexivity is not yet quantified. If Grüns acts on a dashboard signal (pauses spend, ships a retention offer), the training distribution no longer matches the future. This isn't a bug, it's a fundamental limit of acting on predictive signals. Meaningful mitigation requires a held-out non-intervention period to benchmark counterfactuals. Scheduled for Q2.
HIGH
03
iOS attribution gap affects TikTok cohort retention reads. Apple's ATT framework means ~30% of TikTok-sourced subscribers lack deterministic attribution. We triangulate with creator codes, landing-page UTMs, and post-purchase survey "how did you hear about us?" but there's a ±3pp error band on TikTok cohort retention specifically.
MEDIUM
04
Watermelon trial SKU grade is directional. The "C" composite grade is based on 124 reviews and 6 weeks of data, below our 300-review / 10-week threshold for stable grading. The direction (below launch thresholds) is reliable; the specific letter grade could tighten one notch either way once the sample matures.
LOW
05
Google Trends sampling is non-deterministic. Free-tier Trends queries return sampled data; the same query run twice can return slightly different indices. We take a 5-query rolling median to stabilize, but any single search-intent read below a ±8% threshold should be considered noise.
LOW
06
Panel respondents know they're being surveyed. Longitudinal panel design carries a Hawthorne risk: being asked about Grüns monthly may make respondents more aware of the brand than a random consumer. We test this by comparing panel brand-awareness drift against blind awareness studies quarterly; current drift is within noise, but this is a recurring check.
LOW
Any finance model building on these signals should include these caveats in the assumption slide review with data team monthly
