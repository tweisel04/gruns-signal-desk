First-party data (38% of inputs) is the most defensible — no vendor can take it away, no scraping policy can shut it off. Social listening · primaryCRITICAL
Unified mention stream across TikTok, Instagram, Reddit, X, YouTube comments. Brand and competitor tracking with topic classifiers for ingredient mentions, health claims, price discourse, and comparison threads. The single largest input to the Social Velocity and Share-of-Voice metrics.
Feeds these Signal Desk metrics
Social Velocity / Signal Desk Share of Voice / Signal Desk Live signal feed / Signal Desk Social velocity (feature) / Forecast model
Method
Keyword queries (grüns, gruns greens, grüns gummies) plus competitor keyword sets ingested every 5 minutes via Sprinklr API. Sentiment classifier is Sprinklr's proprietary model, validated monthly against a hand-labeled internal test set (see Integrity page confusion matrix — 94% precision). Creator-weighted reach calculated using Sprinklr's follower counts and engagement rate, stripped of bot traffic.
Known limitations
Platform coverage gaps. Sprinklr's TikTok integration is comment-and-post level, not Duet/Stitch-aware — viral remixes of our content may undercount.
Regional bias. Heavily US-English weighted. German-language mentions containing "grüns" (meaning "greens") need a language filter that lives in our pipeline, not theirs.
Sentiment model drift. Retrained by Sprinklr quarterly without notifying customers — we re-run our validation monthly to catch shifts.
Cross-validation / backup
Brandwatch runs parallel ingestion on the same keywords — used weekly to spot Sprinklr blind spots. In a full Sprinklr outage, Brandwatch can carry the Signal Desk alone for up to 30 days with degraded but adequate signal quality (lower recall, higher latency).

Social listening · secondarySUPPORTING
Parallel listening pipeline on the same queries as Sprinklr. Not used as a primary input to any metric — exists specifically to cross-check Sprinklr and to serve as a failover if the primary feed degrades. The ratio of Brandwatch-only mentions to dual-detected mentions is itself a proxy for Sprinklr's recall health.
Feeds these Signal Desk metrics
Listening recall audit / Integrity Source reliability score / Integrity
Method
Same keyword set as Sprinklr, ingested via Brandwatch Consumer Research API. Refreshed every 15 minutes (vs 5-min Sprinklr) — adequate for cross-validation, not adequate to replace Sprinklr for live feed.
Why we pay for two
Single-vendor social listening is a known risk — different platforms grant different vendors different levels of access, and platform policy changes (TikTok in 2024, X in 2023) can cut off a vendor overnight. $18k/yr for dual-sourcing this input is cheap insurance on metrics that drive marketing decisions 10-100× that.

Search intent signalsCRITICAL
Query volume indices for branded, comparative, and cancel-intent search terms. The cancel-intent sub-index is the leading indicator that fires in the Signal Desk alert banner — it reliably precedes actual subscription churn by 14-21 days.
Feeds these Signal Desk metrics
Search Intent Index / Signal Desk Cancel-intent alert / Signal Desk Branded search (feature) / Forecast model
Method
Weighted basket of four query categories: branded (50%), comparative like "grüns vs ag1" (25%), purchase-intent (15%), cancel-intent (10%, inverse-weighted). Accessed via SerpApi for structured returns, with a 5-query rolling median to stabilize Trends' non-deterministic sampling.
Known limitations
Sampling variance. The same query run twice can return slightly different indices — Trends samples, it doesn't aggregate. Our 5-query median smooths this, but changes below ±8% are within noise.
Relative indexing only. Trends doesn't give absolute volumes — everything is normalized to 0-100 within the query window. Makes it hard to say "search volume doubled in absolute terms."
Low-volume queries. If "pause grüns" has insufficient volume in any given week, Trends returns zero. We maintain a floor of 30-day rolling volume to avoid spurious-zero noise.

Longitudinal consumer panel (n=512)CRITICAL
The differentiator of the research stack. Recurring monthly survey of the same 512 consumers, stratified against US census on age, income, region, and dietary identity. Waves 1-14 completed. Longitudinal design — tracking change in the same individuals — is what makes sentiment drift detectable at small effect sizes.
Feeds these Signal Desk metrics
Panel NPS / Signal Desk Composite sentiment gauge / Signal Desk Taste / Efficacy / Value / Trust scores / Signal Desk Substitution map / Signal Desk Panel purchase intent (feature) / Forecast model
Panel design
Screener blinded to brand names during recruitment to avoid Grüns-favorable selection. Post-stratification raking used to correct minor census drift. Each wave runs 10-14 days with email reminders at days 3 and 7. Incentive is a $5 gift card per wave — high enough to sustain engagement, low enough to avoid mercenary respondents.
Known limitations
Hawthorne risk. Panel members know they're being surveyed — their Grüns awareness may drift above a random consumer's. We check quarterly against blind awareness studies.
Wave attrition. 89% wave-to-wave retention is healthy but non-zero. Replacement respondents are stratified-matched to exits.
Single vendor dependency. If Dynata breached contract, rebuilding an equivalent panel takes 3-4 months. Cint maintained as pre-approved secondary.
Contingency
Cint framework agreement signed, $0/mo retainer — activation would cost ~$12k to stand up a 500-person stratified panel within 6 weeks. The longitudinal continuity would break (new panel = new respondents), which is a real cost, but the stack continues to function.

Subscription events & cohort attributionCRITICAL
Every subscription event — signup, first order, flavor selection, skip, pause, cancel, reactivation — captured via Segment and warehoused in Snowflake. This is the ground truth for cohort retention, LTV, and every actual-vs-forecast comparison in the backtest.
Feeds these Signal Desk metrics
M2 retention / Signal Desk Cohort retention curves / Signal Desk LTV:CAC by channel / Retention Room Retention matrix / Retention Room Unit economics waterfall / Retention Room Retention predictors / Retention Room Subscriber journey / Retention Room
Known limitations
iOS 14+ attribution gap. ATT opt-out means ~30% of TikTok-sourced subscribers lack deterministic channel attribution. We triangulate via creator codes, UTM capture, and post-purchase "how did you hear about us" — produces ±3pp error band on TikTok cohort reads specifically.
Retroactive re-attribution. Late-converting multi-touch journeys can get reassigned 7 days after signup. Cohort reads stabilize after day 14.
Why this is the most valuable source
Every other source tells us what consumers say, search, or post. This one tells us what they actually did with their money. No vendor can take it away, no policy change can cut it off, and it's the only source that feeds every single downstream analytics workflow in the company.

Cancel-flow exit interceptSUPPORTING
Single-question survey intercepted in the cancellation flow, asking the primary reason for leaving. Response rate of 38% — high for an exit context. Directly feeds the churn reason breakdown on the Retention Room and the substitution reasons on the Signal Desk.
Feeds these Signal Desk metrics
M2 churn reasons / Retention Room Substitution reasons / Signal Desk
Known limitations
Non-response bias. 62% don't respond — they're probably more annoyed or rushed, which likely underrepresents the "just forgot" category vs what you'd see in ethnographic research.
Single reason only. Forced-choice single answer. Multi-reason churn (e.g., "too expensive AND didn't feel benefit") gets flattened.

Scheduled daily pull of Grüns product reviews from Trustpilot (official integration) and Amazon (Bazaarvoice partnership — Grüns is not direct Amazon seller but appears in third-party listings). Also includes competitor review scrapes for share-of-quality comparison.

Spend philosophy

The stack is deliberately weighted toward signal diversity over single-source depth. Dual-sourcing social listening (Sprinklr + Brandwatch) costs $18k/yr — less than a week of typical paid media — but protects every marketing decision downstream from a single vendor failure.

The Dynata panel is the largest discretionary line item and the one genuine strategic vendor: the longitudinal design is what makes our research more defensible than competitors running ad-hoc Qualtrics surveys. This is the spend to protect in a budget cut.

Internal infrastructure (Segment + Snowflake) is the smallest line because it's leveraged across other data workflows beyond research. Marginal cost attribution to Signal Desk is ~$21k; true fully-loaded cost is higher but shared.
